Lecheflan





Origins And Stories

Leche Flan, a creamy custard pudding, originated from Spain's crema catalana and flan de leche. Filipino cooks adapted this dessert during the Spanish colonial period (16th-19th centuries), making it a staple in local cuisine.

Cultural Significance

Celebratory Dessert : Leche Flan is often served during special occasions like fiestas, weddings and holidays.
Family Gatherings : This dessert brings families together, symbolizing love and unity.
Cultural Heritage : Leche Flan represents Filipino cuisine's rich history and Spanish influence.

Step by step tutorials guide

Ingredients



  • 1. Milk : 2 cups evaporated milk or 1 can (14 oz) condensed milk

  • 2. Eggs : 3 large eggs

  • 3. Sugar :1 cup granulated sugar

  • 4. Vanilla Extract 1 teaspoon

  • 5. Caramelized Sugar1/2 cup granulated sugar (for topping)



    • Equipment



    1). Meduim saucepan
    2). Hand mixer or whisk
    3). 6-8 small molds or ramekins
    4). Steamer or large pot with steamer basket
    5). Refrigerator



    Instructions



    Caramelized Sugar (10 minutes)



    1.) Combine 1/2 cup sugar and 1/4 cup water in a saucepan.

    2.)Heat over medium heat, stirring constantly.

    3.)Once golden brown, remove from heat.

    4.)Pour caramelized sugar into molds.



    Prepare Custard (15 minutes)



    1.) In a separate saucepan, combine milk, sugar and vanilla extract.

    2.) Heat over low heat, stirring occasionally, until sugar

    3.)Remove from heat.

    4.)Let cool slightly.

    5.)Whisk in eggs.

    Pour Custard Into Molds (5 minutes)



    1.)Pour custard mixture into caramelized sugar-lined molds.

    2.)Fill molds about 3/4 full.

    Steam Leche Flab(45-50 Minutes)



    1.) Place molds in a steamer or large pot with steamer basket.

    2.)Steam over boiling water, covered with a lid.

    3.)Check for doneness: edges set, centers slightly jiggly.

    Chill and Serve (4 Hours Or overnight)



    1.) Remove molds from steamer.

    2.)Let cool to room temperature.

    3.)Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.

    4.) Serve chilled.

    Common Issue And Solution

    1.) Cracked Tops Overmixing or excessive heat.

    2.) Watery Texture Undercooked or incorrect egg ratio.

    3.) Burned Caramel Overheating sugar.

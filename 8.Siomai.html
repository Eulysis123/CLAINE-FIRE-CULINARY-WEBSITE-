Siomai





Origins And Stories

Siomai originated from the Chinese dim sum dish, "shaomai," which consists of steamed dumplings filled with pork and shrimp. Chinese traders and immigrants brought this dish to the Philippines, where it evolved and became a staple in Filipino cuisine. Siomai, also known as Filipino steamed dumplings, is a beloved local culinary appetizer in the Philippines. This delicious dish has a rich history, and its evolution is a testament to the country's cultural diversity and culinary creativity.



Filipino Adaption

Filipinos adapted the Chinese shaomai recipe to suit their taste preferences and ingredient availability. They replaced the traditional pork and shrimp filling with a mixture of ground pork, shrimp, and vegetables, such as onions, garlic, and cabbage. The wrapper, typically made from wheat flour, was also modified to be thinner and more delicate.

Cultural Significance

Siomai has become an integral part of Filipino cuisine and culture. It's a popular appetizer or snack served at social gatherings, parties, and celebrations. Siomai is also a staple in many Filipino restaurants and food establishments, where it's often served with a dipping sauce made from soy sauce, vinegar, and chili peppers.






Ingredients

Siomai

- 1 package of siomai wrappers
(usually found in the frozen food section or at Asian markets)
- 1/2 pound ground pork
- 1/2 cup chopped shrimp
- 1/2 cup chopped cabbage
- 1/2 cup chopped scallions (green onions)
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Vegetable oil for brushing





Step by step tutorials guide

Instructions



1. Preparing filling

In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground pork, chopped shrimp, cabbage, scallions, garlic, soy sauce, and oyster sauce (if using). Mix well until all the ingredients are fully incorporated.

2. Seasoned

Season the mixture with salt and pepper to taste.

3. Rest

Cover the mixture and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

4. Assembling siomai

Lay a siomai wrapper on a flat surface. Place a tablespoon of the filling in the center of the wrapper. :


5. Brushing

Brush the edges of the wrapper with a little water.

6. Steaming

Fill a steamer with water and bring to a boil.

7.Steaming (2)

Place the siomai in the steamer, leaving some space between each piece to allow for even cooking.

8. Steaming (3)

Steam the siomai for 15-20 minutes, or until they are cooked through and the wrappers are translucent.

9. Steaming (4)

Serve the siomai hot with your favorite dipping sauce.

Menudo





Origins And Stories

Menudo, a popular Filipino stew, originated from Spanish and Chinese influences during the colonial period. The name "menudo" comes from the Spanish term "menudo," meaning "small" or "little," referring to the dish's bite-sized ingredients. Over time, Filipino cooks adapted and modified the recipe, incorporating local flavors and ingredients.

Cultural Significance

Menudo represents Filipino hospitality, resourcefulness and community bonding. This comforting stew is often served:


  • During special occasions
    (fiestas, birthdays, weddings)
  • Family gatherings and reunions
  • Everyday meals, especially for breakfast or lunch
  • Comfort food during illness or recovery

    Reginald Variations



    Menudo Tagalog
    Original version from Manila, with pork and liver.

    Menudo Pampanga
    Pampanga-style, with added ingredients like tripe and blood.

    Menudo Visaya
    Visayas-style, with fish or seafood.

    Menudo Mindanao
    Mindanao-style, with spicy chili peppers.

    Step by step tutorials guide

    Ingredients

    Protein

    1 pound pork or Beef or chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces

    Liver

    1/2 pound pork or beef liver, sliced

    Vegetables

    - 2 medium onions, chopped
    - 3 cloves garlic, minced
    - 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
    - 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
    - 1 medium carrot, peeled and sliced
    - 2 tomatoes, sliced

    Spices and Seasonings

    - 1 tablespoon fish sauce (Patis)
    - 1 tablespoon vinegar
    - 1 teaspoon salt
    - 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
    - 1/4 teaspoon ground cumin (Optional)



    Instructions



    1. Prepare Ingredients

    Chop onions, garlic, tomatoes, potatoes and carrot.

    2. Saute onions and garlic

    Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Saut√© onions and garlic until softened.

    3. Add Protein

    Add pork, beef or chicken. Cook until browned.

    4. Add Liver

    Add sliced liver. Cook until browned.

    5. Add vegetables

    Add tomatoes, potatoes, carrot and spices. Stir well.

    6. Pour in Broth

    Add beef or pork stock. Bring to a boil.

    7. Simmer

    Reduce heat. Simmer for 30-40 minutes or until meat is tender.

    8. Thicken Sauce

    Mix cornstarch or flour with water. Add to the pot. Stir well.

    9. Season

    Adjust salt, pepper and fish sauce to taste.

    10. Serve

    Serve hot with steamed rice.

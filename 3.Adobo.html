Adobo





Origins And Stories

Adobo, derived from the Spanish word "adobar" Marinade has its roots in Filipino, Spanish and Chinese cuisine. This savory stew originated during the Spanish colonial period (16th-19th centuries) and evolved into various regional variations.

Cultural Significance

Adobo represents Filipino hospitality, resourcefulness and community bonding. This comforting stew is often served:


  • During special occasions
    (fiestas, birthdays, weddings)
  • Family gatherings and reunions
  • Everyday meals, especially for breakfast or lunch
  • Comfort food during illness or recovery

    Reginald Variations



    Adobo Tagalog
    Original version from Manila, with vinegar and soy sauce.

    adobo Pampanga
    Pampanga-style, with added sugar and patis (fish sauce).

    Adobo Visaya
    Visayas-style, with garlic and ginger emphasis

    Adobo puti
    White Adobo, without soy sauce.

    Step by step tutorials guide

    Ingredients

    Protein

    1 pound pork or Beef or chicken,or shrimp (combination)

    Marinade

    - 1/2 cup vinegar (apple cider or white)
    - 1/4 cup soy sauce
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 1 tablespoon black pepper
    - 2 bay leaves

    Aromatics

    - 1 medium onion, chopped
    - 3 cloves garlic, minced
    - 1-inch ginger, sliced

    Spices and Seasonings

    - Salt
    - Pepper
    - Optional: siling labuyo (Filipino chili peppers)

    Oil

    2 tablespoons vegetable oil



    Instructions



    1. Prepare Marinade

    Combine vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, pepper and bay leaves in a bowl.

    2. Marinate Protein

    Add protein to the marinade. Mix well. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes or overnight.

    3. Saute Aromatics

    Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Saut√© onion, garlic and ginger until softened.

    4. Add Protein

    Remove protein from marinade, letting excess liquid drip off. Add to pot. Cook until browned.

    5. Pour Marinade

    Add marinade mixture to pot. Stir well.

    6. Simmer

    Add beef or pork stock. Bring to a boil Reduce heat to low. Simmer for 20-30 minutes or until meat is tender.

    7. Season

    Adjust salt, pepper and vinegar to taste.

    8.Serve

    Serve hot with steamed rice.

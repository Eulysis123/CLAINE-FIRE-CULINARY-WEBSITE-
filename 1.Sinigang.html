SINIGANG





Origins And Stories

Origins and History Sinigang's origins date back to the pre-colonial era, with influences from Malay and Indonesian cuisine. The dish evolved over time, incorporating Spanish, Chinese and American flavors. The name "sinigang" comes from the Filipino term "sigang," meaning "stew" or "soup."

Cultural Significance

Sinigang represents Filipino hospitality, family bonding and community gatherings. It's often served during special occasions like fiestas, birthdays and holidays. This beloved dish embodies the Filipino values of sharing, generosity and respect for tradition.

Step by step tutorials guide

Ingredients

Protein

1 pound pork belly or shoulder, cut into bite-sized pieces (or shrimp, fish, or chicken)

Souring Agents

1 cup tamarind broth (or guava, calamansi, or kamias)

Vegetables

- 2 tomatoes, sliced
- 1 onion, sliced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 ginger, sliced
- 1 cup long beans, cut
- 1 cup eggplant, cut
- 1 cup okra
- 1/4 cup bok choy

Seasonings

Salt
pepper
fish sauce (patis)
shrimp paste (bagoong) (Optional)



Instructions



1. Prepare the Broth

Combine 4 cups water and tamarind broth in a large pot. Bring to a boil.

2. Saute onions and garlic

Heat oil in a pan. Saute onions and garlic until softened.

3. Add Ginger and Protein

Add ginger and protein. Cook until browned.

4. Add Vegetables

Add tomatoes, long beans, eggplant, and okra. Cook for 5 minutes.

5. Combine broth and Protein Mixture

Add sauteed mixture to the tamarind broth. Simmer for 15-20 minutes.

6. Add bok choy and seasoning

Add bok choy, salt, pepper, fish sauce, and shrimp paste (if using).
Simmer for 2-3 minutes.

7. Taste and Adjust

adjust seasoning to taste.

8.Serve

Serve hot with steamed rice.

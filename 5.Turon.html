Turon





Origins And Stories

Turon, derived from the Spanish word "tornar" (to turn), originated in the Philippines during the Spanish colonial period (16th-19th centuries). This crispy spring roll evolved from Chinese lumpia (spring rolls) and Spanish fritter influences.

Cultural Significance

1.)Snack and Appetizer : Turon is a popular snack and appetizer in Filipino gatherings.

2.) Street Food Vendors sell turon in markets and streets.

3.)Family TraditionRecipes passed down through generations.

4.) Celebratory Dish :Served during fiestas, parties and holidays.





Key Ingredients

1.) Saba (Plantain)Bananas : Thinly sliced

2.) Jackfruit Or Lanka : Optional filling

3.)Sugar Sprinkled before frying

4.) Lumpia Wrapper Thin, crispy pastry

5. *Vegetable oil*: Vegetable Oil For frying

Step by step tutorials guide





Instructions



1. Prepare Filling

Slice bananas and jackfruit (if using).

2. Lay limpia Wrapper

Place one wrapper on a flat surface.

3. Add 2-3 banana slices and optional jackfruit.

4. Sprinkle Sugar

Add a pinch of sugar.

5. Fold and Seal

Fold the bottom half, then the sides, and roll up. Seal edges with water.

6. Repeat

Assemble remaining turon.

7. Heat and Oil

Fill the frying pan with 2-3 inches of vegetable oil. Heat over medium-high heat.

8. Fry Turon

Carefully place turon in hot oil. Fry until golden brown (3-4 minutes per side). .

9. Drain Excess Oil

Remove turon oil and drain a tray or plate.

10. Serve

Serve hot, sprinkled with sugar.
